The Cast of Characters

Can move to any green square
The Rook
Honest
Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically. Straight lines only.
No tricks, no surprises. What you see is what you get.

"You sit at the board and suddenly your heart leaps. But what chess teaches you is that you must sit there calmly and think about whether it's really a good idea."— Stanley Kubrick, Film Director

Can move to any green square
The Bishop
Committed
Moves any number of squares diagonally. It never leaves its colour.
Useful — but limited by its own convictions.

"Playing chess is what got me thinking about thinking."— Demis Hassabis · Nobel Prize winner · Creator of AlphaZero

Can move to any green square (27 from centre)
The Queen
Unstoppable
Moves any number of squares in any direction. The Rook and Bishop combined.
From the centre she can reach 27 of the 63 other squares in a single move.

"There is nothing like chess to keep the brain sharp and give it a workout."— Arnold Schwarzenegger

Can move to any green square
The King
Fragile
Moves one square in any direction. Any direction — but only one step.
The most important piece. And yet almost the weakest. Power and importance are not the same thing.

"I've been playing chess since I was very young. It's hard to find someone who wants to play as much as me."— Mo Salah

Can jump to any green square
The Knight
The Trickster
Moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction, then one square perpendicular.
The only piece that can jump over others. The Knight thrives in chaos.

"The best chess move is not the best chess move — it's the move your opponent least wants you to play."— Stephen Fry

Move forward (one, or two at the start)
Capture diagonally
The Pawn
The Underdog
Moves forward one or two squares from its starting position. Captures diagonally.
Every pawn carries a queen inside it.

"Every pawn is a potential queen."— James Mason, Chess Grandmaster

White pawn moves here
Black pawn — can be captured
En Passant
The Ghost Capture
When a pawn advances two squares and lands beside your pawn, you can capture it as if it had only moved one — but only on the very next move.
A ghost capture. Now you see it, now you don't. Miss the moment and the opportunity is gone forever.

"Chess is not about the pieces on the board. It's about what happens between them."— Anonymous

King lands here
Rook lands here
Castling
The Only Team Move
King and Rook move simultaneously. King goes two squares toward a Rook; the Rook jumps to the other side of the King.
The only move where two pieces move at once. Tucks the King to safety and activates the Rook in one move.

"The King is a fighting piece. Use it."— Wilhelm Steinitz, First World Chess Champion

Pawn promotes here — becomes a Queen
Promotion
The Pawn's Dream
When a pawn reaches the other end of the board, it becomes any piece — almost always a Queen.
Every pawn carries a queen inside it. This is the moment it arrives.

"A passed pawn increases in strength as the number of pieces on the board decreases."— José Raúl Capablanca, World Chess Champion
